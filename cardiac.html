Comprehensive Cardiac Screening for Heart Health

This health check includes a comprehensive cardiac screening for heart health. It covers a range of tests to assess and monitor the condition of your cardiovascular system.

Tests Included:

  • Electrocardiogram (ECG or EKG): Measures the electrical activity of the heart, helping to identify irregularities in heart rhythm.
  • Cholesterol Level Test: Measures the levels of different types of cholesterol in the blood, helping to assess the risk of heart disease.
  • Stress Test: Evaluates how well your heart responds to stress, typically through physical exercise, to assess cardiovascular fitness and identify potential issues.
  • Echocardiogram: Uses sound waves to create detailed images of the heart's structure and function, helping to diagnose heart conditions.
  • Blood Pressure Measurement: Checks the force of blood against the walls of the arteries, providing important information about heart health and potential risks.
  • Lipid Profile Test: Measures various types of lipids in the blood, including cholesterol and triglycerides.
  • Cardiac CT Scan: Provides detailed images of the heart and blood vessels to assess heart health.
  • C-Reactive Protein (CRP) Test: Measures inflammation in the body, which can be associated with heart disease.
  • Homocysteine Test: Measures the level of homocysteine in the blood, which can be a risk factor for cardiovascular disease.
  • Treadmill Exercise Test: Evaluates heart function during physical activity to assess cardiovascular fitness.
  • Coronary Angiography: Uses X-rays to visualize the blood vessels supplying the heart.
  • Natriuretic Peptide Test: Measures levels of peptides that indicate heart failure.
  • Carotid Ultrasound: Examines the carotid arteries to assess the risk of stroke and heart disease.
  • Holter Monitor: Records the heart's electrical activity over a period, usually 24-48 hours.
  • Doppler Ultrasound: Uses sound waves to assess blood flow in the arteries and veins.
  • Coronary Calcium Scan: Measures the amount of calcium in the coronary arteries, indicating potential heart disease.
  • B-type Natriuretic Peptide (BNP) Test: Measures a protein that can indicate heart failure.
  • Ambulatory Blood Pressure Monitoring: Monitors blood pressure over a 24-hour period.
  • Thyroid Function Tests: Checks thyroid hormone levels, which can impact heart health.
  • Peripheral Arterial Disease (PAD) Test: Assesses blood flow in the legs and arms.
